"It's not about the money, money, money" - Jessie J.

Yes, quoting a female pop star is a bold move to explain my approach to financial coaching, but hear me out.

Most of the talk around personal finance is centered around money and numbers.

  • "10 ways to build your best budget!"
  • "How to invest for 12% returns!"
  • "15 easy ways to earn $100k!"
  • “Spend less than you make you turd!!"

Okay, I threw that last one in there, but it's to make a point. Most of what is talked about in the world of PF is rooted in “logic” - focusing on numbers, tools, and tactics.

Yet we can’t talk about money without acknowledging the other and often trickier parts of the equation - behaviors, habits, and emotions (like guilt, stress, anxiety) that play an equally large part in our financial lives.

Numbers and tools and tactics are an important piece of the puzzle, but they are only half of it. Just as important is you knowing understanding your motivation behind wanting money.

Why are you building a budget in the first place? What will 15% returns in the stock market get you? How would your life be any more meaningful making $100k than it is now?

That last part - helping people manage their behaviors, habits, and emotions - is what I am very good at and where my value add is. Just like dieting, the main tenet of being financially successful is “make more than you spend.” It’s not rocket science.

Yet the reason most people fail is not because they can’t do addition and subtraction, it’s because they need help discovering their why and what will truly motivate them to do the necessary work.

They need help dreaming big, setting goals, being held accountable and the occasional kick in the ass along the way.
