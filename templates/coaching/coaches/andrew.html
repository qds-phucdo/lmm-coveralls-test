One of the first things I learned when I started to code is how to break down a problem. Most projects seem complex at first glance but once you pick them apart you discover they are really just a bunch of simple steps.

Personal finance is no different only the ultimate goal isn’t to manage your money well, it’s Freedom. Freedom to do things that matter.

While I joke around about “retiring”, it’s not my actual goal. In fact, it’s been well documented that retirement will kill you. The goal shouldn’t be to avoid work, it’s to do meaningful work where you’re in control.

Four years ago I set out to accomplish just that. In that time I built a personal finance resource that every month helps hundreds of thousands of people tackle their money problems. Hours of podcast episodes and hundreds of articles later I’ve been able to build a business that helps educate those in need for free while earning more than I earn at my day job.

Over the course of those four years the most rewarding part by far has been meeting and helping the audience. I believe it’s my life’s work - my way to “make a dent in the universe” (thanks Steve!).

To be clear, I’m not a Registered Investment Advisor and I don’t have a single fancy acronym after my name. I haven’t learned by taking tests, I’ve learned by doing and being educated by hundreds of experts.

Building a business? There are few things that excite me more than talking shop. I’ve learned quite a lot during my journey!

If any of that resonates with you than I’d love to chat.
