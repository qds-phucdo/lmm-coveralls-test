Most of our data is coming from Zillow for the time being but that’s likely to change soon. We think we can get even more accurate data and projections.

In the meantime, we have a data source we’re investigating this will be a much more accurate number soon. Until then we recommend double checking the actual number on Zillow.

Formulas

Estimated Value and Rent Estimate

These values we currently pull from Zillow.com. These values are estimates, although Zillow calls them Zestimates.

Zillow is usually very close but the exact values depend on many local factors. Your milage may vary.

Annual NOI

[Annual NOI] = [Yearly Rent] - [Yearly Property Mgmt] - [Insurance] - [Taxes] - [Yearly HOA Misc]

Net operating income (NOI) is simply the annual income generated by an income-producing property after taking into account all income collected from operations, and deducting all expenses incurred from operations. NOI excludes any financing or tax costs incurred by the owner/investor. In other words, the NOI is unique to the property, rather than the investor.

Gross Yield

[Gross Yield = [Yearly Rent] / [Purchase Price] * 100

Gross Yield is the raw return of the property. It's a great first-step comparison metric.

Capitalization Rate (Cap Rate)

[Cap Rate] = [Annual NOI] / [Purchase Price] * 100

Cap Rate is the annual return on investment without financing. Return if you bought the property in full, in cash.

Cash on Cash

[Cash on Cash] = [Annual Cash Flow] / [Purchase Cost] * 100

Cash on Cash (CoC ROI) is the actual return you will get on this investment. It takes into account all property specific expenses (NOI), the cost of financing and how much you paid for the entire deal.

Cash Flow - Annual

[Annual Cash Flow] = [Annual NOI] - [Yearly Total Mortgage Payment]

Annual Cash Flow is your profit after all expenses. If this number isn't positive that means you'll lose money every year on this property. If it's positive, that's how much you'll earn. This doesn't account for vacancy or things breaking. We do estimate those for you in Cost Assumptions and suggest you hold that money aside just in case.

Cash Flow - Monthly

[Monthly] = [Monthly NOI] - [Mortgage Payment]

Monthly Cash Flow is our monthly profit after all expenses. This is just Annual Cash Flow divided by 12. It's also the sexiest number on this page.

Cash Flow - Medium Term

[Medium Term] = [Monthly NOI] - [Mortgage Payment] - [CapEX Estimate] - [Vacancy Estimate]

Medium Term Cash Flow is an estimation accounting for Vacancy and minor/major repairs. Things break and need to be replaced - we should be prepared. You should strive to be positive here.

Cash Flow - Long Term

[Long Term] = [Monthly Rent] - ([Monthly Rent] * 0.50) - [Mortgage Payment] - [HOA Misc]

Long Term Cash Flow is like the 15% rule only much more pessimistic. In the very long term you may need to replace a roof, renovate a kitchen and account for vacancy. While not necessary, if you are positive here you can weather most any storm and still make money. Formula: 50% of monthly Rent minus the monthly Mortgage Payment.

Cash Flow - First Year Equity

[First Year Equity] = SUM( [First Year Mortgage Principle Payments] )

First Year Equity is how much equity you will gain in the property by making mortgage payments. You can absolutely consider this part of your profit.

Property Taxes (limited estimate)

[Tax Estimate] = [Purchase Price] * 0.01

It's currently a very rough estimate!

Property Insurance (limited estimate)

[Insurance Estimate] = [Purchase Price] / 1000 * 4.6

It's currently a very rough estimate!

Vacancy Rate (limited estimate)

It currently defaults to 5%. In the not too distant future this will be the actual vacancy rate. To get a better estimate, use the US Housing Census Data
